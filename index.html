The Forgotten Renewable

Geothermal energy from deep below the Earth's surface could be a game changer in the transition to clean energy.

By Nick Underwood | March 2020

A vast ocean of heat is just below us. You don't need to fly to Florida or California to find someplace warm. Several football fields below you where you're sitting right now, it's hotter than the Sahara Desert at noon. Less than 5 kilometers below you, the rock is hot enough to boil water. 25 degrees celsius per kilometer on average.
